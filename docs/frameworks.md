# The 10 MAAS™ Frameworks

> Versión en español: [los-10-frameworks.md](los-10-frameworks.md)

The operational composition of MAAS™ — Marketing as a System. Each framework operates a specific layer with its own proprietary metric, installation window, and documented handoff to the brand.

## Architecture

MAAS organizes into **three layers** and **ten frameworks**:

| Layer | Frameworks | What it owns |
|---|---|---|
| **Inner** (Capa Interna) | 1. Factor Be™ | Identity as data |
| **Outer** (Capa Externa) | 2. Persuasion Design™<br>3. Neuronarrative™<br>4. Brand Conversion Messaging™<br>5. Visibility to Value™ | Perception, narrative, conversion |
| **Operational** (Capa Operativa) | 6. Design to Code™<br>7. Design to Convert™<br>8. Growth Loops™<br>9. Automation Goals™<br>10. CRO Ongoing™ | Output, scale, retention |

These three layers map to the **five operable layers of MAAS**:

```
MAAS layer              →  MAAS frameworks
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Perception              →  Factor Be
Narrative               →  Neuronarrative + Persuasion Design
Conversion              →  Brand Conversion Messaging + Visibility to Value
Automation              →  Design to Code + Design to Convert + Automation Goals
Network                 →  Growth Loops + CRO Ongoing
```

---

## 1. Factor Be™ — Real identity diagnostic

**Layer:** Inner
**Question it answers:** *Who is the brand when nobody is watching?*
**Proprietary metric:** Unprompted brand recall + editorial share of voice
**Failure signal:** Customers ask "what do they do?" after visiting the site

The first framework installed. Without Factor Be defined, the rest of the frameworks operate on inconsistent identity. Extracts the brand's differential core — not what the brand claims to be, but what it actually is when nobody is watching.

---

## 2. Persuasion Design™ — Cognitive architecture of the message

**Layer:** Outer
**Question it answers:** *How does the customer's brain process the brand at every contact point?*
**Proprietary metric:** Conversion rate per surface
**Failure signal:** Each landing says something different

Structures how messages get designed at each touchpoint to neutralize specific objections at specific moments. Sequences the brain cannot ignore.

---

## 3. Neuronarrative™ — Narrative architecture

**Layer:** Outer
**Question it answers:** *What story does the brand tell, and how does it sustain it for years?*
**Proprietary metric:** Cross-touchpoint narrative coherence
**Failure signal:** Each email has a different tone — the brand competes against itself

Three levels: manifesto (what the brand believes), positioning (how it differentiates), operational messaging (what it says at each touchpoint). All three must say the same thing at different zoom levels.

---

## 4. Brand Conversion Messaging™ (BCM)

**Layer:** Outer
**Question it answers:** *How does verbal architecture turn attention into measurable purchase intent?*
**Proprietary metric:** Time-to-decision per touchpoint
**Failure signal:** Sales team explains again what was already on the website

Operationalizes narrative for conversion. Matrices of message × cohort × funnel stage.

---

## 5. Visibility to Value™ (V2V)

**Layer:** Outer
**Question it answers:** *How does discovery traffic become qualified pipeline?*
**Proprietary metric:** SQL/visit per channel
**Failure signal:** High traffic, low conversion

A visibility system that doesn't chase empty reach — every impression has conversion architecture behind it. Surfaces work as filter and accelerator simultaneously.

---

## 6. Design to Code™

**Layer:** Operational
**Question it answers:** *How does visual architecture survive technical implementation without loss of intent?*
**Proprietary metric:** Visual drift between design and production
**Failure signal:** The site looks like a watered-down version of the design files

Pixel-perfect with system logic. The layer that ensures the architecture survives the build.

---

## 7. Design to Convert™

**Layer:** Operational
**Question it answers:** *How is every screen designed with one purpose: move the user toward action?*
**Proprietary metric:** CR per surface + drop-off per step
**Failure signal:** High traffic to landing pages, zero on-page action

Applies Persuasion Design + BCM principles directly on interfaces and experience.

---

## 8. Growth Loops™

**Layer:** Operational
**Question it answers:** *How does the brand grow without depending on incremental ad spend?*
**Proprietary metric:** Viral coefficient + organic referral rate
**Failure signal:** If you cut ad spend, conversions drop >70% in 30 days

Designs cycles where each acquired customer feeds the acquisition of the next. Reduces dependency on paid media through organic referral, editorial retention, and partners.

---

## 9. Automation Goals™

**Layer:** Operational
**Question it answers:** *How does the system run without human intervention on the repetitive?*
**Proprietary metric:** Human/automated operation ratio per task
**Failure signal:** The founder is still the bottleneck for editorial output

Automation with purpose — not for efficiency, but so the system runs without a human watching. Every automation has its own KPI and documented handoff.

---

## 10. CRO Ongoing™

**Layer:** Operational
**Question it answers:** *How does the system measure, iterate, and improve every conversion point in perpetual cycles?*
**Proprietary metric:** Quarterly compound lift
**Failure signal:** Metrics flatten after the first quarter post-installation

Continuous optimization. Establishes experiment cadence and handoff to internal Growth team.

---

## Installation cadence

The 10 frameworks install across **3 phases of 30 days** each:

| Phase | Days | Frameworks installed |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 01** — Diagnostic and core install | 1–30 | 1 (Factor Be), 3 (Neuronarrative) |
| **Phase 02** — Layer construction | 31–60 | 2, 4, 5, 6, 7 |
| **Phase 03** — Loops, automation, handover | 61–90 | 8, 9, 10 |

At day 91, Believe withdraws. The system continues operating in the brand's internal team with full documentation.
